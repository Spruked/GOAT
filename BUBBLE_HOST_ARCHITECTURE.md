## 1. Core Principle

Bubble Hosts are **not a governance layer sitting on top of forms**.

Bubble Hosts are the **primary orchestration interface** for all GOAT products.

They **guide**, **coordinate**, and **mediate** between:

* the user
* job-scoped assistants
* Mini-SKGs
* the existing production pipeline
* and **Caleon**, who supervises everything

They **do not replace** the pipeline.
They **do not replace** Caleon.
They **do not remove user control**.

---

## 2. What Bubble Hosts Actually Are

Bubble Hosts are:

* Orchestration shells
* Script-driven interfaces
* Plugin-based connectors
* Job-scoped intelligent workers

### Important Clarification on Intelligence

Bubble Hosts **and their assistants DO have intelligence**, but:

* It is **strictly scoped**
* It is **job-specific**
* It is **non-general**
* It cannot escape its assigned role

They learn **only** how to do *their assigned task* better.

They are **not placeholders only**.
They are **not full AGI**.
They are **competent specialists**.

### Incorrect Prior Assumption (REJECTED)

‚ùå Users never see forms  
‚ùå No buttons

‚ùå No manual input  

**This is wrong.**

### Correct User Interaction Model (LOCKED)

Bubble Hosts **guide** ‚Äî they do not block.

The system supports **multiple interaction speeds**.

### Users MAY:

* Follow conversational guidance
* Fill forms directly
* Use dropdowns and selectors
* Click buttons to proceed faster
* Skip Host prompts (especially repeat users)
* Manually override any non-hard constraint
* Add comments, clarifications, or intent notes
* Mix conversational and form-based input

### Why This Is Mandatory

* Power users move fast
* Repeat users skip guidance
* Precision sometimes beats conversation
* Manual intent must always win

**The Host adapts to the user ‚Äî not the reverse.**
