## 3. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

### Notice Requirements
To submit a valid DMCA takedown notice, you must provide the following information in writing:

#### Identification of Infringing Material
- **Specific Description**: Detailed description of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
- **Location**: URL or specific location of the allegedly infringing material
- **Ownership Proof**: Proof of your ownership of the copyright (registration certificate, etc.)

#### Contact Information
- **Your Name**: Full legal name
- **Your Address**: Complete mailing address
- **Your Email**: Valid email address for communication
- **Your Phone**: Phone number (optional but recommended)

#### Statement of Good Faith
- **Good Faith Belief**: Statement that you believe in good faith that the use is not authorized
- **Accuracy Statement**: Statement that the information in the notice is accurate
- **Perjury Statement**: Statement that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner
- **Signature**: Physical or electronic signature

### Notice Format
- **Written Form**: Notices must be in writing (email acceptable)
- **Complete Information**: All required elements must be included
- **English Language**: Notices should be in English
- **Timely Response**: We respond to complete notices within 24 hours

## 4. DMCA Takedown Process

### Initial Review
- **Receipt Confirmation**: Acknowledgment of notice receipt within 24 hours
- **Completeness Check**: Verification that all required elements are present
- **Preliminary Assessment**: Initial review of the claim's validity
- **User Notification**: Notification to the user whose content is affected

### Content Removal
- **Temporary Removal**: Content removed from public access within 24-48 hours
- **Preservation**: Content preserved for potential restoration
- **Access Prevention**: All access to allegedly infringing content blocked
- **User Communication**: Clear communication about the removal and appeal rights

### Appeal Process
- **Counter-Notification**: Users can submit counter-notifications within 10 days
- **Content Restoration**: Content may be restored if valid counter-notification received
- **Copyright Owner Rights**: Copyright owners can seek court injunctions
- **Legal Resolution**: Disputes resolved through legal channels if necessary

## 5. Counter-Notification Process

### Counter-Notification Requirements
To file a counter-notification, you must provide:

#### Identification
- **Your Name**: Full legal name
- **Your Address**: Complete mailing address
- **Your Email**: Valid email address
- **Your Phone**: Phone number

#### Content Identification
- **Material Description**: Description of the material that was removed
- **Location**: Original location of the material before removal
- **Restoration Request**: Request for restoration of the material

#### Statement of Good Faith
- **Good Faith Belief**: Statement that you believe in good faith that the material was removed in error
- **Authorization**: Statement that you are authorized to act on behalf of the content owner
- **Consent to Jurisdiction**: Consent to federal court jurisdiction in your district
- **Service Acceptance**: Acceptance of service of process from the complaining party

### Counter-Notification Process
- **Submission**: Send counter-notification to our DMCA agent
- **Forwarding**: We forward your counter-notification to the original complainant
- **Content Restoration**: Content restored within 10-14 days unless legal action taken
- **Legal Protection**: You may be protected from liability for misrepresentation
